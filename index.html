Most expensive cities in the world to live in

The most important cities in the world are often also the most expensive to live in.
Below, we will discuss the top five most expensive cities to live in.

Discover here

Why live in a city?

Living in a city often has its advantages. For instance, there is good public transport which means you no longer need a car. Additionally, a lot of different cultures come together, shops and various other things are easy to reach and there are plenty of job opportunities.

Top 5 most expensive cities in the world

By 2022, the most expensive cities in the world are:

  • 1. New York
  • 2. Singapore
  • 3. Tel-Aviv
  • 4. Los Angeles
  • 5. Hong Kong

    • New York

    New york has been declared the most expensive city in the world to live in. This is because the united states has suffered from high inflation over the past year, which has also caused rents and house prices to rise.


    The graph on the right shows the average selling prices of houses in Manhattan's neighbourhoods. In the Nolita neighbourhood, houses sell for an average of $6,000,000. And in Little Iitaly, it is a lot less with an average of $2,800,000.

    Singapore

    Together with New York city, the city of Singapore ranks an first place on the most expensive cities in the world list. The map shows Singapore's most expensive street. This street is called Nassim Road.
    Some of the most expensive houses in all of Singapore are located here.

    Tel Aviv

    Tel Aviv ranks third when it comes to the most expensive cities in the world. This puts the city down two places compared to last year. The most expensive neighbourhood in Tel Aviv is Tzamarot Ayalon, which is followed by the areas of Tzahala and Neve Tzedek.

    Los angeles

    Suprising is that Los Angeles only ranks fourth. You would think that with all the expensive neighbourhoods like Hollywood and Bel- Air, the city would be higher on the list. As with New York, inflation in the United States has caused the high house prices in the city. Los Angeles did rise in the ranking compared to 2021. Back then, the city ranked 9th.


    The map shows the percentage of homes rented or purchased. For Los Angeles County, 54.4% of homes are rented more than the 16.4% that are bought.

    Hong Kong

    Along with Los Angeles, Hong Kong shares the fourth spot.
    In 2019, 2020, 2021, Hong Kong ranked in the top three.
    As with the other cities, inflation is a big factor in the price increases.


    The map shows the five most expensive houses in Hong Kong.
    In 2021, a residential flat at Mount Nicholson sold for $82m.
    At the time, this broke the record for the most expensive flat in the whole of Asia.

    The chart shows the average house prices of the world's top five. What stands out is that New York house prices go for much less than Singapore and Hong Kong. This is due to the surface area of the homes. In New York, the area of a house is often much smaller than that in Singapore. It is also notable that in all the cities, house prices have increased significantly. With the highest increase being in Singapore.


    We have highlighted the three most expensive buildings in the New York city to live in. 35 Hudson Yards is the most expensive building in the city. Some of the penthouses housed in this building have been listed around US$59m in 2020.


    With high inflation over the past year, it is no surprise that prices are increasing by this much. Apart from that, it has also become increasingly attractive for people to live in the city.
